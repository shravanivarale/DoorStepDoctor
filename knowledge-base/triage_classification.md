# Triage Classification System

## Overview

Triage is the process of determining the priority of patients' treatments based on the severity of their condition. This system helps ASHA workers decide who needs immediate care and who can wait.

---

## TRIAGE CATEGORIES

### 🔴 RED - EMERGENCY (Immediate)

**Definition:** Life-threatening conditions requiring immediate medical attention

**Time to Treatment:** IMMEDIATE (within minutes)

**Transport:** Ambulance (108) or fastest available transport

**Examples:**
- Unconscious or unresponsive
- Not breathing or severe difficulty breathing
- No pulse or very weak pulse
- Severe bleeding (uncontrolled)
- Chest pain (suspected heart attack)
- Severe burns (>10% body surface area or face/hands/genitals)
- Seizures (ongoing or not recovering)
- Signs of shock (pale, cold, weak pulse, confusion)
- Severe head injury with altered consciousness
- Suspected spinal injury
- Obstructed labor
- Cord prolapse
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Poisoning with altered consciousness
- Severe dehydration with shock
- Respiratory distress with cyanosis (blue lips)
- Stroke symptoms
- Severe abdominal pain with shock
- Eclampsia (seizures in pregnancy)
- Postpartum hemorrhage (severe bleeding after delivery)

**Vital Signs Indicating RED:**
- Respiratory rate >30 (adult) or >50 (infant)
- Heart rate >120 (adult) or <60 (adult)
- Blood pressure <90/60 or >180/120
- Temperature >106°F or <95°F
- SpO2 <90%
- Unresponsive or responds only to pain (AVPU: P or U)

**Action:**
1. Call 108 ambulance immediately
2. Start first aid while waiting
3. Monitor vital signs continuously
4. Prepare for transport
5. Do NOT delay for any reason

---

### 🟡 YELLOW - URGENT (Within 1 Hour)

**Definition:** Serious conditions that need medical attention soon but are not immediately life-threatening

**Time to Treatment:** Within 1 hour

**Transport:** Arrange transport to PHC promptly

**Examples:**
- High fever (>103°F / 39.5°C)
- Moderate dehydration
- Fractures (closed, not life-threatening)
- Severe pain (not life-threatening)
- Persistent vomiting (not severe dehydration yet)
- Moderate burns (<10% body surface area, not critical areas)
- Suspected pneumonia (fast breathing but no severe distress)
- Prolonged labor (>12 hours)
- Fever in infant <3 months
- Suspected malaria or dengue
- Moderate bleeding (controlled but significant)
- Severe diarrhea (not yet severely dehydrated)
- Suspected appendicitis
- Eye injury or sudden vision loss
- Severe allergic reaction (controlled, no breathing difficulty)
- Diabetic with very high or very low blood sugar (conscious)
- Suspected fracture of major bone (femur, pelvis)
- Severe abdominal pain (no shock)
- Breech presentation in labor
- Retained placenta
- Moderate respiratory distress

**Vital Signs Indicating YELLOW:**
- Respiratory rate 25-30 (adult) or 40-50 (infant)
- Heart rate 100-120 (adult)
- Blood pressure 140-180/90-120
- Temperature 103-106°F
- SpO2 90-94%
- Responds to voice but confused (AVPU: V)

**Action:**
1. Provide first aid and stabilization
2. Arrange transport to PHC within 1 hour
3. Monitor vital signs every 15-30 minutes
4. Watch for deterioration to RED category
5. Give clear instructions to family

---

### 🟢 GREEN - NON-URGENT (Within 24 Hours)

**Definition:** Minor conditions that can wait for regular clinic hours

**Time to Treatment:** Within 24 hours

**Transport:** Can use regular transport, no rush

**Examples:**
- Low-grade fever (<103°F) with no danger signs
- Minor cuts and wounds
- Mild diarrhea (no dehydration)
- Common cold or mild cough
- Skin rashes (not severe)
- Minor sprains or strains
- Mild headache
- Minor burns (first-degree, small area)
- Ear pain (no fever or discharge)
- Sore throat (no difficulty breathing)
- Mild abdominal pain
- Constipation
- Minor allergic reactions (no breathing difficulty)
- Toothache
- Minor eye irritation
- Mild nausea (no vomiting)
- Insect bites (no allergic reaction)

**Vital Signs Indicating GREEN:**
- All vital signs within normal range
- Temperature 99-103°F
- No respiratory distress
- Alert and oriented (AVPU: A)
- SpO2 >95%

**Action:**
1. Provide basic first aid
2. Give home care instructions
3. Advise to visit PHC within 24 hours
4. Educate on danger signs to watch for
5. Advise when to return immediately

---

### 🔵 BLUE - ROUTINE (Scheduled Care)

**Definition:** Chronic conditions, preventive care, and follow-up visits

**Time to Treatment:** Scheduled appointment

**Transport:** Regular transport, no urgency

**Examples:**
- Chronic disease follow-up (diabetes, hypertension)
- Immunizations
- Antenatal checkups (routine)
- Growth monitoring
- Family planning
- Health education
- Medication refills
- Routine physical examination
- Nutrition counseling
- Postnatal checkups (routine)
- Well-child visits
- Chronic cough (stable, under treatment)
- Skin conditions (chronic, stable)

**Action:**
1. Schedule appointment at PHC
2. Provide health education
3. Ensure medication compliance
4. Monitor for any changes
5. Encourage regular follow-up

---

## TRIAGE DECISION TREE

### Step 1: Check for RED (EMERGENCY) Signs

**Ask yourself:**
- Is the patient breathing?
- Is there a pulse?
- Is the patient conscious?
- Is there severe bleeding?
- Are there signs of shock?

**If YES to any:** Classify as RED

### Step 2: Check for YELLOW (URGENT) Signs

**Ask yourself:**
- Is there high fever?
- Is there moderate pain?
- Is there moderate bleeding?
- Is there dehydration?
- Is there difficulty breathing (not severe)?

**If YES to any:** Classify as YELLOW

### Step 3: Check for GREEN (NON-URGENT) Signs

**Ask yourself:**
- Are symptoms mild?
- Are vital signs normal?
- Is patient alert and active?
- Can this wait 24 hours?

**If YES to all:** Classify as GREEN

### Step 4: If None of Above

**Classify as BLUE (ROUTINE)**

## SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### Infants (<3 Months)

**Lower threshold for emergency:**
- ANY fever = YELLOW or RED
- ANY difficulty breathing = RED
- Poor feeding = YELLOW
- Lethargy = RED

### Pregnant Women

**Lower threshold for emergency:**
- ANY fever = YELLOW
- Abdominal pain = YELLOW
- Vaginal bleeding = RED
- Severe headache = YELLOW
- Seizures = RED

### Elderly (>60 Years)

**Lower threshold for emergency:**
- Confusion = YELLOW or RED
- Falls = YELLOW
- Chest pain = RED
- Shortness of breath = YELLOW or RED

### Chronic Disease Patients

**Lower threshold for emergency:**
- Diabetics with altered consciousness = RED
- Hypertensives with severe headache = YELLOW
- Asthmatics with breathing difficulty = YELLOW or RED

---

## DANGER SIGNS (Always RED)

**Airway and Breathing:**
- Not breathing or gasping
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Choking
- Cyanosis (blue lips/fingernails)

**Circulation:**
- No pulse
- Severe bleeding
- Signs of shock
- Chest pain

**Consciousness:**
- Unconscious
- Responds only to pain
- Seizures
- Severe confusion

**Other:**
- Severe burns
- Severe trauma
- Poisoning with symptoms
- Severe allergic reaction

---

## TRIAGE IN MASS CASUALTY SITUATIONS

### START Triage (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment)

**Step 1: Walking Wounded**
- Ask everyone who can walk to move to a designated area
- These are GREEN (non-urgent)

**Step 2: Assess Remaining Patients**

**Check Breathing:**
- Not breathing after opening airway = BLACK (deceased)
- Breathing >30/min = RED
- Breathing <30/min = Continue assessment

**Check Circulation:**
- No pulse or capillary refill >2 seconds = RED
- Pulse present and capillary refill <2 seconds = Continue assessment

**Check Mental Status:**
- Cannot follow simple commands = RED
- Can follow simple commands = YELLOW

---

## DOCUMENTATION

**Record for each patient:**
1. Triage category (RED, YELLOW, GREEN, BLUE)
2. Time of triage
3. Chief complaint
4. Vital signs
5. Danger signs present
6. Action taken
7. Time of transport (if applicable)

---

## RE-TRIAGE

**Important:** Patient condition can change

**Re-assess:**
- Every 15 minutes for RED patients
- Every 30 minutes for YELLOW patients
- Every 1-2 hours for GREEN patients

**Upgrade category if:**
- Condition worsens
- New danger signs appear
- Vital signs deteriorate

**Downgrade category if:**
- Condition improves
- Danger signs resolve
- Vital signs stabilize

---

## COMMUNICATION

### To Patient/Family

**For RED:**
"This is an emergency. We need to get to the hospital immediately."

**For YELLOW:**
"This is serious and needs medical attention soon. We should go to the PHC within the hour."

**For GREEN:**
"This is not an emergency, but you should see a doctor within 24 hours."

**For BLUE:**
"This can be managed with a routine appointment. Let's schedule a visit to the PHC."

### To Receiving Facility

**Provide:**
1. Triage category
2. Chief complaint
3. Vital signs
4. Danger signs
5. Treatment given
6. Estimated time of arrival

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Under-triaging:** Classifying serious condition as non-urgent
   - Always err on side of caution
   - When in doubt, upgrade category

2. **Over-triaging:** Classifying minor condition as emergency
   - Can overwhelm system
   - But better than under-triaging

3. **Not re-assessing:** Failing to check if condition changes
   - Always re-triage regularly

4. **Ignoring vital signs:** Relying only on symptoms
   - Always check vital signs

5. **Delaying RED patients:** Waiting for transport
   - Use any available transport for RED patients
