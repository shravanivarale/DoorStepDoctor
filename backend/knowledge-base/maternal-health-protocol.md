# Maternal Health Triage Protocol

## Document Information
- **Source**: National Health Mission - Maternal Health Guidelines
- **Version**: 3.0
- **Last Updated**: January 2024
- **Category**: Maternal Health

## Overview
This protocol guides ASHA workers in assessing pregnant women and new mothers in rural settings.

## Emergency Conditions (Immediate Referral)

### During Pregnancy
- **Severe bleeding** (soaking > 2 pads per hour)
- **Severe headache** with blurred vision or spots before eyes
- **Convulsions or seizures**
- **Severe abdominal pain**
- **High fever** (> 101째F) with chills
- **Sudden swelling** of face, hands, or feet
- **Decreased or no fetal movements** (after 28 weeks)
- **Gush of fluid** from vagina before 37 weeks
- **Severe vomiting** with inability to keep fluids down
- **Difficulty breathing** or chest pain

### During Labor
- **Heavy bleeding**
- **Prolonged labor** (> 12 hours for first baby, > 8 hours for subsequent)
- **Baby's hand or foot coming first**
- **Umbilical cord visible** before baby
- **Mother exhausted** and unable to push
- **Convulsions**
- **High fever** during labor

### After Delivery (Postpartum)
- **Heavy bleeding** (soaking > 2 pads per hour)
- **Severe abdominal pain**
- **High fever** (> 100.4째F)
- **Foul-smelling vaginal discharge**
- **Severe headache** with vision changes
- **Breast infection** with fever
- **Difficulty breathing**
- **Severe depression** or thoughts of harming self/baby

**Action for ALL Emergency Conditions**: 
- Call 108 immediately
- Arrange immediate transport to hospital
- Do NOT delay for any reason
- Keep mother lying down
- Monitor vital signs

## High Priority (Same Day Assessment)

### Pregnancy Warning Signs
- Moderate bleeding or spotting
- Persistent headache
- Fever 100-101째F
- Burning sensation during urination
- Severe nausea/vomiting
- Reduced fetal movements
- Swelling of feet/ankles
- Unusual vaginal discharge

### Postpartum Warning Signs
- Moderate bleeding
- Fever 99-100째F
- Breast pain or engorgement
- Difficulty urinating
- Constipation with pain
- Mood changes or sadness
- Difficulty bonding with baby

**Action**: Refer to PHC within 4-6 hours. Monitor closely.

## Medium Priority (PHC Visit Within 24-48 Hours)

### Pregnancy Concerns
- Mild nausea/vomiting
- Mild swelling
- Backache
- Constipation
- Heartburn
- Mild headache
- Fatigue

### Postpartum Concerns
- Mild bleeding (normal lochia)
- Mild breast discomfort
- Mild perineal pain
- Mild constipation
- Mild fatigue
- Questions about breastfeeding

**Action**: Schedule PHC visit. Provide guidance and support.

## Routine Antenatal Care Schedule

### First Trimester (0-12 weeks)
- Register pregnancy
- First ANC visit
- Blood tests (Hb, blood group, HIV, HBsAg)
- Urine test
- Weight and BP measurement
- Tetanus vaccination (TT1)

### Second Trimester (13-28 weeks)
- Monthly ANC visits
- Monitor weight gain
- Check BP
- Fetal heart monitoring (after 20 weeks)
- Tetanus vaccination (TT2)
- Iron and folic acid supplementation

### Third Trimester (29-40 weeks)
- Bi-weekly visits after 36 weeks
- Monitor fetal movements
- Check for swelling
- Birth preparedness counseling
- Identify delivery location

## Danger Signs Education

### Teach Every Pregnant Woman
1. Bleeding during pregnancy
2. Severe headache with vision changes
3. Convulsions
4. Severe abdominal pain
5. Fever with chills
6. Decreased fetal movements
7. Water breaking before time
8. Prolonged labor

### Emergency Preparedness
- Identify nearest facility for delivery
- Arrange transport in advance
- Save money for emergency
- Identify blood donor
- Keep emergency contact numbers
- Plan for childcare for other children

## Postpartum Care (First 6 Weeks)

### Day 1-3
- Check for bleeding
- Monitor temperature
- Assess breastfeeding
- Check baby's condition
- Provide emotional support

### Week 1
- Home visit by ASHA worker
- Check healing of perineum/cesarean wound
- Assess breastfeeding
- Check for postpartum depression signs
- Provide family planning counseling

### Week 6
- Postpartum checkup at PHC
- Assess complete recovery
- Family planning counseling
- Immunization schedule for baby

## Breastfeeding Support

### Normal Breastfeeding
- Baby feeds 8-12 times per day
- Baby has 6+ wet diapers per day
- Baby gaining weight
- Mother comfortable during feeding

### Breastfeeding Problems (Refer to PHC)
- Cracked or bleeding nipples
- Severe breast pain
- Hard, red, hot breast (mastitis)
- Baby not latching properly
- Baby not gaining weight
- Insufficient milk production

## Nutrition During Pregnancy and Lactation

### Recommended Diet
- Extra meal per day
- Iron-rich foods (green leafy vegetables, jaggery)
- Protein (dal, eggs, milk)
- Fruits and vegetables
- Adequate water (8-10 glasses)
- Avoid tea/coffee with meals

### Supplements
- Iron and folic acid tablets (IFA)
- Calcium tablets
- Vitamin D (sunlight exposure)

## Red Flags for ASHA Workers

### NEVER Ignore
1. Any bleeding in pregnancy
2. Severe headache in pregnancy
3. Convulsions
4. Fever in pregnancy or postpartum
5. Decreased fetal movements
6. Prolonged labor
7. Heavy postpartum bleeding
8. Suicidal thoughts

## Documentation Requirements
- Pregnancy registration
- ANC visit dates
- Weight and BP measurements
- Test results
- Complications noted
- Referrals made
- Delivery outcome
- Postpartum visits

## Important Reminders
- **DO NOT** recommend medications
- **DO NOT** conduct deliveries at home
- **ALWAYS** refer complications to PHC
- **ENCOURAGE** institutional delivery
- **PROVIDE** emotional support
- **RESPECT** cultural practices (if safe)
- **MAINTAIN** confidentiality
